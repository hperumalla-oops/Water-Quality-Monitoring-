Water Quality Monitoring System

Why Monitoring Water Quality is Important

Monitoring water quality is crucial for ensuring the health and safety of our communities. It helps in identifying potential contaminants, pollutants, and maintaining a sustainable environment. Regular monitoring allows for prompt response and effective management of water resources, ensuring access to clean and safe water for all.

pH

Definition: Measure of how acidic or basic water is.

Importance: pH affects the solubility and biological availability of chemical constituents in water.

Turbidity

Definition: Cloudiness or haziness of a fluid caused by large numbers of individual particles.

Importance: Indicates water clarity and can affect light penetration and aquatic life.

Total Dissolved Solid

Definition: Measure of all inorganic and organic substances contained in a liquid in molecular, ionized, or micro-granular suspended form.

Importance: High TDS levels can indicate poor water quality and may affect taste and safety.
